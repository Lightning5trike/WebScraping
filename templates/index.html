Fibres

When choosing yarn for a project it is important to consider what qualities you are looking for in a yarn. Below the 3 main types of yarn have been outlined and from here you can decide what types of yarn you would like for your project.

Animal Fibres

Animal fibers are known for their warmth and insulating properties but are avoided by vegan makers or those with certain allergies.

They are typically very warm, very soft, breathable, and quite stretchy. On the negative side, they can also pill and felt, and are not machine washable.

Examples: Sheep wool, alpaca, yak, mohair, angora, cashmere, camel, qiviut, slik.

Plant Fibres

Plant fibers require some processing to turn raw materials into yarn. While plant fibers share commone qualities like moisture-wicking and easy care, they also share some negative traits, like heaviness and inelasticity.

They are usually not very warm, can absorb a lot of water, some of them have an excellent stitch definition, and they are often machine washable. They are not very stretchy and can be more difficult to knit with.

Examples: Cotton, linen, nettle, bamboo.

Synthetic Fibres

Synthetic fibers are made using chemical processes rather than naturally-occurring substances. While typically easier to find and care for, more makers are considering the environmental impact of synthetic yarns before purchase.

Often very inexpensive, quite soft, and quite versatile. But not breathable at all, and often not the highest comfort factor.

Examples: Nylon, Acrylic, Polyamid.
